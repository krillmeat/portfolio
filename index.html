KRILL MEAT

Krill Meat is a personal brand I have been building over the past few years. I have been doodling in the margins for years, gathering sketchbooks, notepads, and lots of post its. When coworkers started asking for the illustrations I had on my desk, I decided to move forward with the idea of promoting, branding, and selling my work.

Krill Meat embodies funny, simple, sometimes jarring, often uncomfortable, consistently messy illustrations. I wanted to make sure the website worked not only as an online store, but also a portfolio to showcase my work, so the art is front-and-center.

Code

For this site, I wanted to use some kind of subtle animation technique to create a sense of unease with the illustrations. To do that, I needed to keep all of the SVG files seperated and inline, so I could manipulate various pieces. To do this without cluttering up the HTML, I pulled all of the svg data into a JS object as Strings, and used JS to inject them into the page. From there, it's simple CSS Animations to create the effects.

Beyond the SVG management, I also wanted to include a simple swiping carousel on mobile. I built a custom JS plugin that integrated with the overall page manager to control the effect on mobile.

ABSORB

View the Website

The Walker Insights series is an annual conference, held in Minneapolis and covers a variety of design topics. Each year, a selection of designers are picked to speak about their work and process, and answer some questions from the audience. ABSORB is a hypothetical 2021 conference, created by gathering three existing lectures from earlier years and tieing them up into one cohesive concept.

The challenge was to discover a connecting theme between the three lectures, all held in three unique years, and put them together in a believable way. The concept of ABSORB came from talking points found in all three lectures. From there, the goal was to create a cohesive brand for that theme, and use that brand to create advertising, collateral, and presentation resources.

Color

The color choice of black and white was made because of the nature of the event: a lecture series from three different artist. Because these artists will be showcasing their own work, and will not be involved in the particular branding, the surrounding designs should allow the presenter's work to take the center stage, without any colors clashing.

  • Dark#060606

  • Light#D9D8D3

Typography

Rubik

Subheadings and Body Copy

I chose Rubik because I wanted something that worked well at any scale. I also wanted something with rounded edges, small counters at higher weights, and is blocky, which I felt fit the ominous but playful theme of “ABSORB”. Even though it gets more “soft” and “playful” at very thick weights, with smaller counters and rounded edges, the typeface is still legible at smaller font sizes and looks clean without being sterile.

IMPACT

Impact is a hypothetical Science/Art Exhibit designed to help people learn more about their carbon footprint and it's impact in their communities, while playing games and making art.

Visitors fill out a quiz about their personal habits, which in turn will generate a game of Minesweeper. Play the game, and their results will produce a poster, which is hung together with others on a gallery wall. By the end of the exhibition, the hope is to fill the wall with posters, creatign one large mural: a collage of ecological impact.

SERVELLO

Servello is a skin care brand that focuses on natural, organic products. The company’s inception came from the memories of home remedies in an old, Italian kitchen. To echo the emotion of memory, while keeping things feeling natural, the direction the brand took was delicate and pristine, without losing the warmth of nostalgia.

Servello may be a small company, selling organic and natural products, but the owner’s background in biology sets it on a higher level than the others in its field. Servello focuses on keeping you looking and feeling good, without the lasting damages that may come with other skin care products, all while staying smart and natural.

Color

The colors were all chosen to reflect the atmoshpere that Servello cultivates with their promise to create all-natural products.

  • Olive#6B705C

  • Artichoke#A5A58D

  • Champagne#FFE8D6

  • Pine#DDBEA9

  • Pasta#CB997E

Typography

Matao

Montserrat

Matao's high-contrast stroke weights and mix of casual and high-class feel mesh well with the brand of Servello: refined, yet accessible. Montserrat was chosen to be a neutral font to pair easily with the statement font, and works well to help reinforce a clean and professional attitude.

Packaging

A big part of the brand for Servello is tradition meets modernity. I wanted to pull inspiration from classic labels, especially for apothecary bottles, and trim down the detail to allow for a more modern look and feel.

I wanted to create a small interactive effect for the background "pattern" on the larger hero sections. This code makes the patterns move around in relation to the mouse on the hero section.

function handleCoolMouse(e){

let t = document.getElementById("hero");

// Grab the center of the element, and its relationship to the mouse position

let hCenter = t.offsetWidth / 2;

let vCenter = t.offsetHeight / 2;

let deltaX = (e.clientX - t.offsetLeft) - hCenter;

let deltaY = (e.clientY - t.offsetTop) - vCenter;

// I don't want the offset to ever go past a certain point (200px)

if(deltaX > 200){ deltaX = 200 }

else if(deltaX < -200){ deltaX = -200 }

if(deltaY > 200){ deltaY = 200 }

else if(deltaY < -200){ deltaY = -200 }

// Go through each piece of the pattern and offset them

for(let i = 0; i < heroBlobs.length; i++){

let translate = `translate(${-1*(deltaX/4)*heroBlobs[i].dataset.offset}px,${-1*(deltaY/4)*heroBlobs[i].dataset.offset}px)`;

heroBlobs[i].style.transform = translate;

}

}

ATTAGIRL

Cullum's Attagirl is a fried chicken and beer restaurant located near the N St. Mary Strip in San Antonio. The area is known for its bars, restaurants, and murals, and has cultivated a trendy but down-to-earth attitude over the last few years.

The Attagirl restaurant and its atmosphere have a sarcastic attitude. They have a strong voice with their menu, and they know what they are. But while the restaurant and food feel like a great fit, the branding feels left out. The goal was to update the Attagirl brand to better match their overall tone, as well as freshen things up to keep up with competitors in a quickly-growing "fancy fast food" industry.

Logo

Color

The colors need to be simple and playful, and "mostly" duotone. The range of the reds help give some depth, but the stark contrast between them and the dark blue help give it a more of a "colorized black and white" feeling.
